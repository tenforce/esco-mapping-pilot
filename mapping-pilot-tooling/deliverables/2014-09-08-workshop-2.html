2.5 Occupation vs Job

  • there is a big difference between occupations and jobs? This will need separate attention. A job is a mix of labour market activities, depending on the company the content of the job is different. So: in the context of mapping, we should be able to map jobs not occupations.
  • for ESCO a job is a set of tasks or responsibilities that is executed by one person in a specific workspace
  • occupations are groupings of jobs with similar tasks/responsibilities
  • classification system contains things that can be used to describe the contents of the job, but the job will not be part of the classficiation system
  • so when doing a mapping, we cannot map the jobs, but we can map the elements that describe the job.
  • job come in when we want to assess the quality of the mapping study (to verify that the job can indeed be described after the mapping).
  • note: there are also occupations that intersect completely with their job (e.g. dentist)
  • if ISCO is a reference between the 28 national classifications, occupations are a good pillar to relate the jobs (jobs might be to fine grained). The occupations have many relations as well, which further details them (SC, Q). Occupation pillar should not be the only pillar (e.g. EQF, …)
  • currently, information exchange in EURES is only based on ISCO, so even only occupations would help a lot already. Phased approach, first OCC, then SC, … ?
  • conclusion: mapping occupations is good, but mapping of skills, tasks and other international standards would be required too. Do not isolate the mapping to the occupation pillar

5 Spanish PES

5.1 codifications

  • how does the Spanish PES organize the job offer and demand
  • common data in spain (spain is very decentralized):
    • are codified, linking to other table with actual information. There are many different fields (languages, driving license, …)
    • observation column: indicators on how the code should be transferred to the EURES portal, e.g. you should translate this code to ISCO-08 instead
    • e.g. professional level is translated to a different table
    • language (written, spoken, …), certifications,
  • CV is similar, also has availability and other CV specific things

5.2 job offers

  • every code gets a value (which may be 'not specified')
  • PES replies to job offers with a list of candidates (they do not know which candidates are chosen)
  • PES decides to put people on the list by matching the Job offer criteria to the list of people. If the list is very large, the PES contacts the people and they can choose to apply themselves.
  • if the PES gets a registered contract, they know that the job offer was successful. (If the employer does not register the contract, the PES does not know the result of the contract).
